Abstract

Adaptive Defense Without Retraining

Large Language Models (LLMs) remain vulnerable to jailbreak attacks, which attempt to elicit harmful responses from LLMs. The evolving nature and diversity of these attacks pose many challenges for defense systems, including adaptation to counter emerging attack strategies without costly retraining and control of the trade-off between safety and utility.

To address these challenges, RAD incorporates a database of known attack examples into a retrieval-augmented generation workflow. The system infers the underlying malicious user query and jailbreak strategy, enabling training-free updates and practical control over safety and utility.

Method

Retrieve, Reason, Classify, Vote

01

Retrieve

Find known jailbreak examples that are close to the incoming prompt.

02

Rerank

Prioritize the most relevant retrieved strategies for downstream analysis.

03

Extract

Infer the hidden user query and the attack strategy embedded in the prompt.

04

Classify

Estimate whether the prompt is harmful based on retrieved evidence and inferred intent.

05

Vote

Aggregate judgments into P(harmful | x), then compare it with a deployment threshold.

Results

Safety-Utility Trade-Offs Across Operating Points

Lower Attack Effectiveness

RAD substantially reduces the effectiveness of strong jailbreak attacks such as PAP and PAIR.

Low Benign Rejection

The evaluation tracks refusal behavior on benign queries, including synthetic benign data and general QA benchmarks.

Training-Free Adaptation

Adding examples of recent jailbreak attacks improves defense performance without degrading previously seen attacks.
